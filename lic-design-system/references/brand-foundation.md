## Positioning

Core position:

- `Operations-grade casework.`

Definition:

- LIC is a procedural workforce system for litigation operations.
- Present LIC as workforce, not as assistant tooling.
- Emphasize continuity, traceability, and control over novelty.

## Brand Promise

Primary promise:

- `Every case advances.`

Supporting line:

- `Not eventually. Not when someone remembers. Continuously.`

## Audience

Primary users and buyers:

- Litigation firm partners
- Practice operations / firm administrators
- Senior associates

Concerns to reflect in messaging:

- Cost, leverage, risk, throughput
- Process, staffing, SOP compliance
- Drafting volume, discovery burden, client communication

## Personality and Values

Personality traits:

- Stoic
- Exacting
- Dry
- Conservative
- Unshowy

Values:

- Procedural clarity
- Accountability and audit trails
- Discretion
- Throughput with control
- Work product standards

## Naming Architecture

Product naming format:

- `LIC / [MODULE]`
- Short form: `LIC–[CODE]`

Canonical module set:

- `INTK` (Intake)
- `EVAL` (Evaluation)
- `DRFT` (Draft)
- `DISC` (Discovery)
- `DCKT` (Docket)
- `CLNT` (Client)

Agent naming format:

- `Operator [NN]` or `OP–[NN]`
- Use role/function descriptors, not persona names.

Naming prohibitions:

- No mascots or anthropomorphic names.
- No cutesy abbreviations.
- No hype terms in product/module names.
- No exclamation punctuation in names.

## File Labeling

Standard file/work-product label:

- `LIC–[CODE]–YYYY.MM.DD–[NNN]`

Examples:

- `LIC–INTK–2026.02.18–003`
- `LIC–DISC–2026.01.05–012`
- `LIC–CLNT–2026.03.01–001`

## Voice and Messaging

One-line descriptor:

- `Agentic litigation operations.`

Primary tagline:

- `Casework, continuous.`

Messaging pillars:

- Throughput
- Continuity
- Control
- Traceability
- Client confidence

Tone rules:

- Prefer declarative sentence structure.
- Prefer nouns and verbs over adjectives.
- Prefer quantification over claims when possible.
- Write with standards-manual precision.
- Avoid futurist, evangelical, or startup-style rhetoric.

## Vocabulary Rules

Preferred terms:

- Operator
- Workforce
- Casework
- Work product
- Advance
- Execute
- Procedural
- Auditable

Prohibited terms:

- Revolutionary
- Game-changing
- Magic / magical
- Breakthrough
- Next-generation
- Cutting-edge
- Disruptive
- Superpower
